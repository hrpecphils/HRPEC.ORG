OUR WORK

At HRPEC, we are dedicated to advancing the cause of human rights through a variety of initiatives and programs. Our work is rooted in the belief that the arts, education, and dialogue are powerful tools for promoting human rights and empowering individuals and communities.

MIYERKULET!

MiyerKULET! is a regular Wednesday event, providing a safe space for the arts and embracing the belief that arts are a crucial aspect of human existence. It serves as a consistent virtual and physical platform for artists, filmmakers, musicians, and creators to freely present and discuss their work without financial constraints.

MIYERKULET!

MiyerKULET! is a regular Wednesday event which has been a safe space for the arts, rooted on the belief that the arts are one of the many frontiers of human existence. It serves as a consistent virtual and physical space where art practitioners, filmmakers, musicians, and other creators are able to present and discuss their ideas and their work to an audience, free of charge or the commercial limitations of hosting their art.

Within a year, MiyerKULET! has been staged over 50 times, showing over 70 films and 10 theater performances to over 2000 attendees. This event partners with different organizations to help promote human rights through a variety of media, and, more importantly, offers a safe space for all HR advocates and allies to converse freely on human rights issues and discuss strategies to further the HR agenda in their own spaces.

The main priority of the event is to ensure that perspectives are given, heard, and processed as a group. The artwork–in most cases, static or already finished–serves to facilitate possible discussions among the spectators.

What works for the staging of MiyerKULET! are the conducive conditions which the HRPEC office offers. MiyerKULET! is held at a well-ventilated open-air rooftop, with a panoramic view of Quezon City. Its space is constantly changing and evolving with its growing community, always making room for new faces and ideas.

This diverse community, with its open minds and spirited discussions, is the centerpiece of MiyerKULET!.

SINE SIYUDAD

Sine Siyudad is a community-based film screening event organized in collaboration with local stakeholders, organizations, and institutions. Its main objective is to stimulate meaningful discussions and reflections among the audience by showcasing thought-provoking films within the context of Philippine social realities and human rights.

SINE SYUDAD

Sine Siyudad is a community-based film screening event held in collaboration with local stakeholders, organizations, and institutions.

The program aims to foster meaningful discussions and reflections among the audience by featuring thought-provoking films. These discussions are facilitated within the context of Philippine social realities and human rights.

Sine Siyudad has successfully taken place in various communities across Metro Manila, including Brgy. Krus na Ligas, Brgy. Bagong Silangan, Brgy. Payatas, and Brgy. Tumana. While HRPEC spearheads the program in different locations, our ultimate goal is to empower local organizations and communities with the necessary skills to independently sustain and manage Sine Siyudad.

This includes providing support in marketing strategies and logistical arrangements. By empowering local communities and organizations, we hope to encourage ongoing dialogue and engagement on human rights in the Philippines.

HRPEC CHIKAHAN

HRPEC Chikahan is a monthly roundtable discussion that brings together a diverse group of participants, including experts, activists, policymakers, and community representatives, to address critical human rights issues.

This inclusive platform provides an opportunity for open and constructive dialogue, facilitating the exchange of ideas, experiences, and perspectives on promoting and advancing human rights practices.

Its launch, entitled "HRPEC Chikahan: A Roundtable Discussion on LGBT+ and Women's Human Rights", focused on shedding light on the barriers and challenges faced by LGBT+ individuals and women in accessing their human rights. It facilitated participatory discussions that allowed both the panelists and audience members to address these issues together, in an effort to build a more just and inclusive society that upholds the rights of all individuals.

The roundtable discussions aim to develop actionable recommendations and steps that the human rights community can collectively undertake to strengthen human rights frameworks. These recommendations will be made available as a shared study among human rights organizations and institutions.

HUMAN RIGHTS DISCUSSION

HRPEC's Human Rights Education lectures and seminars, which have reached over 170 venues, play a crucial role in fulfilling the organization's goals.

HRPEC firmly believes that education and early awareness of one's rights lead individuals to a lifetime of questioning, exploration, and commitment to upholding the innate rights we all possess.

Over the past five years, HRPEC has provided its Human Rights Education lectures and seminars at virtually no cost to audiences willing to listen and engage in discussions about the state of human rights in the Philippines.

Today, HRPEC's Human Rights Discussions adopt a listener's perspective and embrace the growing awareness of individuals' rights and the circumstances in their respective communities that affect the realization of those rights.

Rather than simply informing audiences about their rights, HRPEC's discussions focus on cultivating a realization of the presence of both the facilitator's and the audience member's rights and how they can be continuously upheld. Inclusivity and participatory community-building are at the core of these discussions, aligning with the formulation of ideas about human rights.

Since these discussions aim to expand and reach wider audiences, the training of trainers for Human Rights Discussions is essential for success.
